Help
Getting started ▼
What is Assistant Coach? ▼
Assistant Coach is a football play-tracking and analytics tool designed for coaching staffs, built to work on phones and tablets. It does three things:

1. Tag game film — Pull in Hudl cutups and break down plays with your own terminology.

2. Track plays live — Use it on the sideline during games to record every snap in real time, tap by tap.

3. Analyze everything — Visualize your data with charts, stat breakdowns, and an AI assistant that answers questions about your plays in plain English.
What can I actually do with this app? ▼
Here's everything at your fingertips:

Live Game Tracking — Record downs, formations, play types, results, and more with customizable tap-to-tag columns. Auto-saves every tap.

Custom Templates & Columns — Build your own tracking sheets. Create columns with the exact buttons your staff needs — offense, defense, special teams, scout, whatever you want.

AI-Powered Spotlight — Load any game and the AI instantly surfaces the biggest offensive and defensive takeaways. No searching through data — it tells you what matters.

Ask AI Anything — Type a question like "What's our 3rd down conversion rate in the red zone?" and get an instant answer with real numbers from your data.

Visual Stat Dashboards — Full breakdowns of run/pass splits, down & distance tendencies, formation efficiency, hash tendencies, quarter-by-quarter performance, and more.

Playbook Context — Teach the AI your play names, formations, personnel groupings, and terminology so it speaks your language.

Export & Backup — Export games to Excel spreadsheets. Back up everything to your device or Google Drive. Move data between devices seamlessly.

Voice Input — Ask the AI questions using your voice — useful when your hands are full on the sideline.
Is this for game days or film study? ▼
Both. On game day, open the Game Tracker and tag plays in real time from the sideline or the press box. During film sessions, load your Hudl cutups into Stats & Analysis and dig into what the data shows — formations, tendencies, efficiency, all of it.
How can this help my coaching staff? ▼
Replace paper charts and scattered spreadsheets with one tool that does everything. After a game you can instantly answer questions like: What was our 3rd down conversion rate? Which formation gained the most yards? Where did our defense struggle? The AI handles all the number crunching — just ask in plain English and get your answer.
🏈 TRACKING PLAYS ▼
How do I start tracking a game? ▼
From the home screen, tap Game Trackers → New Game Tracker. Pick a template (this sets up your columns), enter the team names if you want, then tap START GAME. The tracking sheet opens and you're ready to go.
How do I record a play? ▼
Each row is one play. Tap the buttons in each column to record what happened — the down, formation, play type, result, whatever your template tracks. When the play is over, tap + Rows to advance to the next row.
I made a mistake — how do I fix it? ▼
Tap ← Prev to go back to the play you need to correct. Tap the right buttons — they'll overwrite the old values. Then hit + Rows to return to where you were. If you want to completely wipe a play, tap ✕ ROW in the header bar.
How do I jump to a specific play? ▼
Tap the play number chip in the bar at the top of the tracking sheet. A dialog pops up — type the play number and tap GO.
Do I need to save my game? ▼
Nope — every tap auto-saves instantly. When you're done, just tap ← Back and your game will be in the Archive whenever you need it.
How do I export a game to Excel? ▼
Inside a game, tap ⬇ XLSX in the header. Choose to save the file to your device or to Google Drive. From the Archive, you can also select multiple games and export them all into one spreadsheet.
Templates & COLUMNS ▼
What are columns and templates? ▼
A column is one category you track per play — like "Formation" or "Result." Each column has a set of buttons with the options you can tap. A template is a saved layout of columns that you use for a game. You might have separate templates for offense, defense, or scout film.
How do I create a new template? ▼
Go to Game Trackers → Templates → + NEW. Name your template, then tap columns from the available list to add them. Drag to reorder. Tap CREATE TEMPLATE when you're done.
How do I create a custom column? ▼
Go to Game Trackers → Templates → COLUMNS → + NEW. Give the column a name, then tap ＋ ADD BUTTON to add each option coaches can tap during tracking. Save it, and the column becomes available in any template.
How do I reorder columns or switch templates during a game? ▼
In the Template Builder, press and hold any column in the selected list and drag it to reorder. During a live game, tap the template chip in the sheet bar to switch to a different template — your play data stays intact.
How do I save the current game layout as a template? ▼
Inside a tracker, tap + TMPL in the header. Give it a name and save. It'll show up in your template list for future games.
Stats & analysis ▼
How do I see stats for my games? ▼
From the home screen, tap Stats & Analysis → FILTER ▼. Select the games or cutups you want to analyze — you can filter by season, home/away, conference, or Big 3. Check the ones you want, then tap LOAD →.
What is the Spotlight tab? ▼
⚡ Spotlight fires automatically every time you load data. The AI reads through all your plays and surfaces the most important findings — things like a high 3rd-down conversion rate, or a formation that's consistently losing yards. Each insight has a ? button to explain how it was calculated, and 👍/👎 buttons to tell the AI what's useful to you.
How do I ask the AI a question about my plays? ▼
Tap the ASK AI tab. Make sure data is loaded (use FILTER first), then type your question the same way you'd ask an assistant coach — "What's our 3rd down conversion rate?", "Which formation gains the most yards?", or "How did we do in the red zone?". You can also use the 🎤 button to ask by voice.
What are the different Stats sub-tabs? ▼
OVERVIEW — total plays, yards per play, and your run vs. pass split.

OFFENSE — down & distance breakdowns, formation efficiency, 3rd down conversions, and play type tendencies.

DEFENSE — yards allowed, opponent run/pass tendencies, sacks, TFLs, interceptions, and stop rate.

FIELD — hash mark tendencies, red zone efficiency, and field position data.

BY QTR — quarter-by-quarter performance for both offense and defense.
Can I save an AI response for later? ▼
Yes — tap the 📌 SAVE button underneath any AI answer. It gets stored in the 📌 SAVED tab so you can reference it later — during halftime adjustments, post-game meetings, or weekly prep.
⚙️ SETUP & SETTINGS ▼
How do I teach the AI my play names? ▼
Go to Settings → Run Plays or Pass Plays. Type your play names and what they mean — one per line, like: Bolt = Counter Left. Now when you ask the AI about "Bolt," it knows exactly what play you're talking about. You can do the same for formations, tags, and terminology.
How do I set up my team info? ▼
Go to Settings → Team Info. Enter your team name, school, and rivalry opponents. Once saved, the AI will know who you are and can automatically filter for rivalry games when you ask about them.
How do I back up my data? ▼
Go to Settings → Save Full Backup. Choose Save to Device to download a .json file, or Save to Drive for Google Drive. Do this regularly, especially before switching devices. Your entire setup — games, templates, columns, and AI settings — gets saved.
How do I move data to a new phone or tablet? ▼
Save a backup to Google Drive on your current device (Settings → Save to Drive). On the new device, open the app and go to Settings → Restore → From Drive. Everything carries over — games, templates, playbook settings, all of it.
My cutup list seems outdated — what do I do? ▼
The app caches your cutup list for up to 24 hours to speed things up. If you've recently added new cutups and they're not appearing, go to Settings → Hudl Data Cache → Clear Cache. The next time you open the filter, it will pull a fresh list.
